## Overview: 
A (planned to be) Semiautonomous drone designed to sample otherwise inaccesible parts of weather systems utilizing a novel wing folding design allowing rapid dives through adverse wind conditions that would otherwise damage or destroy a conventional aircraft or drone.

### Current Features: 
Total BOM cost of less that 200 dollars (support equipment excluded)

A full length "keel" and internal flexible spars to allow flight even after heavy damage 

A foldable wing design built using simple components, mainly steel hinges, fishing line, and a 20kg strength servo motor

A payload bay with support for temperature, humidity and barometric pressure sampling as well as a camera for visual observation.

Onboard Data processing via Arduino

Oversized full moving combination Elevator, rudder, and aileron control surfaces for extreme control authority in all phases of flight

A modular design built around individual sections fitted together on 2mm steel rods.

### In Progress features: 
A bleed air system to allow even greater takeoff performance with wings extended and flight control with wings retracted

Additional modules, including a mechanism to allow launch from other flight platforms

### Planned Features: 

Deformable wings to allow greater control authority 

Autonoumous "Return to home" capability in case of signal loss

Wind speed and direction monitoring, as well as precipitation detection.

## Purpose
As it stands, the only way to collect data on the inside of storms is via expensive and complex doppler radar systems, this project aims to change that with an accessable and cheap platform by which to gather data at the source

## Notes
Assembly is straight forward, the rods pass through 3 identical holes on each part and then all parts snap together using the inbuilt pegs.
See the electronic mounting locations for information on intended mounts for non printed components.
